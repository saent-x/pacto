# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pacto serves mobile-first users organizing shared life at different social scales: Solo for individuals, Pairs for couples, and Crews for multiple people. Users are on iOS and Android, often checking in quickly, adding tasks or memories in the moment, and returning later to coordinate plans, reminders, check-ins, wishlists, and everyday logistics.

## Product Purpose

Pacto helps people coordinate daily life without making care work feel like admin work. It brings practical workflows such as tasks, plans, reminders, memories, and emotional check-ins into one expressive mobile app. Success means users can see what matters today, add or update shared items quickly, understand who is involved, and feel that the app reflects the relationships and routines behind the data.

## Brand Personality

Playful, expressive, distinctive. The interface should feel alive and personal without becoming childish, sentimental, or noisy. It should make ordinary coordination feel warmer and more memorable while preserving speed, clarity, and trust for repeated daily use.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboard patterns, overly cute couple-app tropes, dark glassy AI-app styling, and uninspiring design. The product should not look like a corporate task manager, a pastel relationship scrapbook, a neon assistant shell, or a conventional admin app compressed onto mobile.

## Design Principles

Make coordination feel human: keep the emotional context of shared life visible without turning workflows into greeting-card sentiment.

Scale cleanly from Solo to Pairs to Crews: every surface should make ownership, audience, and shared state legible without assuming a couple-only model.

Favor fast capture and easy recovery: mobile users should be able to add, adjust, undo, and resume quickly with minimal typing and clear feedback.

Use personality as structure: expressive type, color, and motion should clarify priority, state, and meaning rather than decorate generic layouts.

Protect daily trust: reminders, memories, and shared records need clear states, predictable controls, and no surprise data loss.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The product is mobile-app only for iOS and Android. Design decisions should support native mobile accessibility expectations: readable text, clear focus and screen-reader labels, adequate contrast, large touch targets, reduced-motion-friendly animation, and one-handed use where practical. Because Solo, Pairs, and Crews are all first-class modes, copy and UI should avoid couple-only assumptions.
